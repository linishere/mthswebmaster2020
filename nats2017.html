National Convention 2017 at Orlando

The year of 2017 saw the MTHS TSA’s largest participation and most successful finish at the national competition. Nine students and two advisors attended the conference in Orlando, hopeful to make appearances on stage. Students carefully packed their projects, including Matthew Khajehpour, who built a large crate to hold his Animatronics team’s alligator, preventing any possible damage. After an aspiring three days, two teams made semi-finals in their events: Sophie Burbank and Reiden Chea in Structural Design and Engineering, as well as Reiden Chea, Matthew Khajehpour, Robert Krieger, and Reece Newhouse in Animatronics. After a rigorous semi-final competition consisting of a building challenge for Structural Design and Engineering as well as a LEAP interview for both events, two Terrace teams stood on the national stage. Sophie Burbank and Reiden Chea won the Structural Design and Engineering National Championship, and the Animatronics team took 9th place. The achievement of these two teams continues to inspire members within the MTHS TSA chapter to strive for success, putting forth their best efforts.
